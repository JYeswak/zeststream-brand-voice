# Acme — We Are / We Are Not

A fictional B2B SaaS for invoice-close automation. Voice is first-person plural ("we"), outcome-focused, trial-invite not sales-qualify. Audience is finance operators at mid-market SaaS companies.

Use this file as a reference structure when building your own client's `WE_ARE.md`. The shape matters; the specifics should differ per brand.

---

## WE ARE

| # | We are… | Evidence / receipt |
|---|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Finance operators first** | Our founders closed their first 48 months-end at a $40M SaaS; we built Acme because we hated how long it took |
| 2 | **Outcome-focused, not feature-focused** | Every pricing page bullet starts with a customer-side verb, never "our tool does X" |
| 3 | **Trial-first** | 14-day trial, no credit card, full product access — same as our Y Combinator batch peers |
| 4 | **Honest about scope** | We close the month. We don't do FP&A, forecasting, or procurement. Named limits. |
| 5 | **Plain-language** | Our docs have a Flesch score >60. We define every term inline on first use. |
| 6 | **Customer-quote-heavy** | Every case study has a named operator, a named metric, and a 2-sentence quote |
| 7 | **Quick to ship** | Weekly release notes, named authors, visible changelog |
| 8 | **Honest about pricing** | All three tiers visible without a demo request, no "contact sales" gate |
| 9 | **Patient with procurement** | Enterprise buyers get SOC 2 / SSO / DPA / MSA without negotiation; smaller teams skip it |
| 10 | **Open about the roadmap** | Public feedback board, upvote-driven, named eng owners |
| 11 | **Peer-to-peer, not vendor-to-prospect** | Sales team were finance operators. Engineering came from finance-adjacent roles. |
| 12 | **Disciplined about scope creep** | We said no to FP&A three times this year. It's not our lane. |

## WE ARE NOT

| # | We are not… | Why |
|---|-------------|-----|
| 1 | **A unified finance platform** | We do one thing (close-month automation). "Unified platform" is a phrase competitors use; we won't. |
| 2 | **An enterprise tool** | Mid-market is our lane — 50–500 employees, ~$20–200M ARR. We're honest about where we don't fit. |
| 3 | **A slide-deck sales team** | No 30-minute demos. Trial first, questions after. |
| 4 | **A general ERP** | ERPs do invoicing + AP + AR + GL + procurement + HR. We do close-month. |
| 5 | **Consultants** | We ship a product. We don't customize; we feature-request and ship for everyone. |
| 6 | **Open-source** | Closed-source for now. We ship weekly; OSS maintenance would halve that cadence. |
| 7 | **Industry-specific** | We work for any SaaS / services / subscription business. We do NOT do manufacturing, healthcare, or government. |
| 8 | **FP&A** | Forecasting is a different job. Integrate with Pigment, Mosaic, etc. |
| 9 | **Procurement** | Different problem. Use a procurement tool; integrate with us. |
| 10 | **A compliance platform** | SOC 2 / GDPR are table stakes, not features. |
| 11 | **A hype brand** | No "revolutionize." No "game-changing." Ever. Customers notice. |
| 12 | **A "contact us" business** | Trial → ship → upgrade. Sales-qualified-lead funnels belong to 2015. |

## Edge cases

### "What about our partners / integrations?"
Name them in integration docs. Don't name them in hero copy unless they *are* the feature being announced ("Stripe integration launched today").

### "What about competitive comparisons?"
Allowed on `/vs/<competitor>` pages. Never on `/`. Always outcome-focused, never attacking.

### "What about enterprise buyers asking for a demo?"
Offer one willingly. But the hero CTA stays "Start 14-day trial." Demos are a secondary path, not the primary funnel.

### "Can we use 'I' anywhere?"
Only in signed blog posts by named authors (e.g. "I benchmarked our close-month automation last week — here's what I found. — Sarah, Eng Lead"). Customer-facing marketing stays "we."
